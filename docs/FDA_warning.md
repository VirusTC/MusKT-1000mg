Clinical Warning Statement & Adverse Reaction Monograph
-------------------------------------------------------

Product Focus: MusKT™ 1000mg (Standardized USP-Grade *Myristica fragrans* Supercritical Extract)\
Protocol Status: Mandatory Patient Safety Addendum\
Classification: Organic Investigational Antioxidant Adjunct (6-Month Compassionate Use Framework)

* * * * *

I. Boxed Warnings & Critical Alerts
-----------------------------------

1\. Central Nervous System Toxicity (Myristicin Overload)
---------------------------------------------------------

MusKT™ 1000mg contains naturally occurring volatile phenylpropanoids, specifically myristicin and elemicin. Strict adherence to the authorized once-daily 1000mg threshold is mandatory. Exceeding this dose or accumulating the compound due to hepatic clearance failure can induce acute central nervous system hyperexcitability. Symptoms include toxic psychosis, visual and auditory hallucinations, acute delirium, severe muscle rigidity, and generalized tremors.

2\. Hepatic Metabolic Saturation Risk
-------------------------------------

The active terpenoids and volatile organic compounds within MusKT™ are cleared exclusively via hepatic pathways. Prolonged, consecutive daily administration over a 6-month window carries a strict risk of competitive enzyme inhibition. If a patient's metabolic clearance slows down, it can cause the compound to build up in the bloodstream, increasing the risk of systemic toxicity.

A. Central Nervous System & Neurological
----------------------------------------

-   Psychoactive Alterations: At the documented 1000mg therapeutic dose, mild central nervous system symptoms like transient dizziness, lightheadedness, or slight spatial disorientation may occur during the first 14 days of initialization.
-   Autonomic Stimulation: Patients may occasionally experience mild restlessness, dry mouth, or transient sinus tachycardia (resting heart rate spikes up to 90--100 bpm) shortly after ingestion.

B. Hepatic & Enzyme Pathways
----------------------------

-   Cytochrome P450 Competition: The constituent plant-derived matrices compete for shared microsomal enzyme clearance pathways in the liver. This can temporarily slow down the clearance of other co-administered botanical structures or medications.
-   Transaminase Elevations: Mild, transient increases in serum Alanine Aminotransferase (ALT) and Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST) may occur. Any steady increase tracking toward ≥ 2× the Upper Limit of Normal (ULN) requires an immediate, permanent hold on the product.

C. Gastrointestinal Dynamics
----------------------------

-   Gastric Mucosal Irritation: The natural volatile oils can irritate sensitive stomach linings if taken on an empty stomach. This can cause mild nausea, warm epigastric sensations, or localized abdominal cramping.

* * * * *

III. Contraindications & Exclusion Criteria
-------------------------------------------

MusKT™ 1000mg is strictly contraindicated in patients presenting with any of the following pre-existing clinical baselines:

1.  Decompensated Hepatic Disease: Baseline transaminase levels exceeding > 1.5× the Upper Limit of Normal (ULN), active hepatitis, or a history of hepatic cirrhosis.
2.  Pre-existing Neurological or Psychiatric Conditions: A clinical history of epilepsy, unmanaged seizure disorders, severe chronic anxiety, schizophrenia, or clinical psychosis.
3.  Pregnancy and Lactation: Due to potential uterine-stimulating properties associated with certain volatile botanical fractions, use is entirely precluded in pregnant or nursing patients.

* * * * *

IV. Immediate Emergency Discontinuation Thresholds
--------------------------------------------------

The patient or caregiver must immediately suspend MusKT™ administration, seek emergency medical evaluation, and notify the Principal Investigator if any of the following emerge:

-   Neurological Alterations: Acute confusion, unsteadiness, unprovoked agitation, picking at imaginary objects, or heart palpitations exceeding > 110 bpm at rest.
-   Gastrointestinal Distress: Severe, persistent vomiting or an inability to retain fluids.
-   Hepatic Changes: The onset of unexplained jaundice (yellowing of the eyes or skin), dark or tea-colored urine, or severe right-upper-quadrant abdominal pain.
